About Our Project

The Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) Detection project is dedicated to leveraging advanced AI and machine learning technologies to facilitate early detection of ASD. Our mission is to empower caregivers, educators, and healthcare professionals with tools to identify behavioral and speech patterns associated with ASD, enabling timely interventions and support.

We aim to bridge the gap between technology and healthcare by providing accessible, accurate, and user-friendly solutions that enhance the quality of life for individuals with ASD and their families.

Our Mission

To revolutionize ASD detection through innovative AI solutions, ensuring early identification and personalized support for individuals and communities worldwide.

Our Vision

To create a world where early ASD detection is seamless, accessible, and integrated into everyday healthcare and educational systems, fostering inclusivity and support for all.

Our Project Mission

Objective

The primary objective of this project is to develop an intelligent and accessible system for the early detection of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) using deep learning techniques. By leveraging Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) such as ResNet18, EfficientNetB1, and EfficientNetB2, the project aims to identify distinctive facial features associated with ASD from biomedical and social media images.

Problem Statement

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a complex neurodevelopmental condition that often remains undiagnosed in early childhood due to limited access to specialized diagnostic tools and reliance on subjective behavioral assessments. Traditional diagnosis methods are time-consuming, costly, and prone to human bias, leading to delayed intervention.

Scope of Project

The scope of this project encompasses the development of a deep learning-based system capable of detecting Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) through facial image analysis. The system utilizes advanced Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) architectures such as ResNet18, EfficientNetB1, and EfficientNetB2 to identify subtle facial patterns associated with ASD.
